Long before the rise of [[the Imperium]] or the First Empire of Man, [[the Aetherian]] held sway over vast territories. This empire was founded by the Aether-Touched, a lineage of humans with traces of celestial ancestry.

The Aether-Touched were almost like demigods, gifted with heightened sensitivity to the world's subtle energies. This sensitivity manifested as an innate talent for magic, exceptional longevity, and piercing insight. Their celestial heritage was evident in their striking appearance - pale, almost luminous skin, and eyes that seemed to hold the depth of the night sky.

The Aetherian was built on a sophisticated understanding of natural philosophy and magic. They excelled in astronomy, mathematics, and alchemy. Their cities were marvels of architecture and engineering, featuring towering observatories, vast libraries, and intricate aqueduct systems that some claim were powered by more than mere gravity.

The Aetherians' rule was marked by a complex bureaucracy and a meritocratic system where magical aptitude and scholarly achievement were highly valued. They sought to cultivate these gifts in the general population, establishing academies and magical institutions throughout their lands.

However, the Aetherian's downfall came not from hubris, but from within. As the celestial bloodline thinned over generations, a schism formed between purists who wished to preserve their dwindling power and progressives who wanted to further integrate with the general population. This internal conflict, combined with external pressures from neighboring powers, led to a protracted civil war that shattered the empire.

In the aftermath, much of the Aetherians' advanced knowledge was lost or fragmented. The surviving Aether-Touched scattered, their descendants gradually blending into other populations. Today, individuals occasionally surface with unusual magical aptitude or longevity, hinting at a distant Aetherian ancestry.

Remnants of Aetherian culture persist in ancient ruins, arcane texts, and certain magical traditions. Scholars and mages still study these remnants, seeking to unravel the secrets of the Aether-Touched and their once-great civilization.
